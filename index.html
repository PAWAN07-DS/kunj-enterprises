Why Go Solar?

Your electricity bill is rising.
Solar is the smarter choice.

With solar, you generate your own clean energy — cutting bills, boosting property value, and protecting the environment for generations.

💰

Cut Your Electricity Bill by 80–90%

A well-sized solar system can virtually eliminate your monthly electricity bill. Most customers recover their investment in 4–6 years and enjoy free electricity for 20+ years after.

🏛️

Central & State Government Subsidies

Eligible residential customers can receive subsidies under PM Surya Ghar Yojana and state schemes. We handle all documentation and subsidy applications on your behalf.

⚡

Earn from Extra Electricity

With an on-grid system, your surplus power feeds back into the grid — and you earn credits against your bill through net metering, making your meter run backwards.

🌿

Zero Carbon Footprint

Switch to clean, renewable energy and reduce your carbon footprint significantly. Each kW of solar installed saves approximately 1.5 tonnes of CO₂ annually.

🏡

Increase Property Value

Homes and commercial properties with solar installations command a premium in the real estate market. Solar is now a top-listed feature for buyers in urban UP.

🔧

Low Maintenance, Long Life

Solar panels have no moving parts and require minimal upkeep. With panels warranted for 25 years and inverters for 5–10 years, it's the most reliable long-term investment.

FAQ

Common questions,
honest answers.

How much does a 3 KW solar system cost? ▼
A 3 KW solar system from Kunj Enterprises is priced at ₹2,10,000. Installation charges are ₹2 per watt, which adds ₹6,000 for a 3 KW system. Eligible residential customers may also receive a government subsidy, which can significantly reduce the net cost. We offer transparent pricing with no hidden charges.
Is government subsidy available for solar in UP? ▼
Yes. Residential customers in Uttar Pradesh are eligible for subsidies under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana. The subsidy amount depends on the system size (e.g., up to ₹78,000 for 3 KW). As a government-impanelled vendor, we assist you with all required documentation and subsidy applications at no extra cost.
Which brands of solar panels do you use? ▼
We use panels and inverters from India's most trusted brands: Tata Power Solar, Adani Solar, Waaree, Havells, Ksolare, and Microtek. All are Tier-1, BIS-certified panels. We do not use unbranded or substandard equipment, because your system is a long-term investment.
How long does the installation take? ▼
A typical residential installation (3–5 KW) takes 1–2 working days once all approvals are in place. The full process from site visit to net meter connection usually takes 3–4 weeks, depending on DISCOM approval timelines. We keep you informed at every step.
What warranty is provided with the system? ▼
Solar panels carry a 25-year performance warranty (typically 80% output at 25 years) and 10-year product warranty from the manufacturer. Inverters come with a 5-year warranty. Our workmanship and installation is covered for 1 year. We also provide ongoing after-sales support and annual maintenance services.
Which areas do you serve? ▼
We currently serve Lucknow, Sitapur, and Gorakhpur, including surrounding districts and villages. If you are based in a nearby location, please call or WhatsApp us — we may be able to accommodate your project depending on feasibility.
What is net metering and how does it benefit me? ▼
Net metering allows you to export surplus solar electricity back to the DISCOM grid. The excess units are credited to your account, which offset future consumption — effectively making your meter run backwards. This maximises the financial return from your solar investment, especially for residential and commercial customers.
Is there any maintenance required after installation? ▼
Solar panels require minimal maintenance — mainly periodic cleaning to remove dust and bird droppings, which can reduce output by 10–20% if left unattended. We recommend cleaning every 2–3 months. We offer annual maintenance packages, or you can simply clean with water yourself. Inverters should be inspected annually.
